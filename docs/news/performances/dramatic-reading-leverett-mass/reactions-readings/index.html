Reactions to Readings

Full House, November 22, 2013, Leverett, MA

  Some reactions from readers and audience members

to readings of the Project Unspeakable full script.

“Deep gratitude to all involved in putting this together. Hope it spreads far and wide. So relevant to today’s context. I’m in my 30s and didn’t have any memory of the assassinations. Though the script was about events that happened 40-50 years ago, it felt like time had collapsed. Very inspiring to learn of the courageous acts of great leaders of the past. I’m inspired to find that courage within myself to also help others find theirs to seek the truth.” – Lopez Island, WA

“Most striking [were] the love of these 4 primary brothers of the time: MLK, JFK, RFK, and Malcolm X; and the deep sense of gratitude and the call to courage that the play evoked.” – Lopez Island, WA

“The play is powerful. We all look forward to doing it again.” – Lopez Island, WA

 “The subject matter and script has a very high emotional charge, esp. for people who are old enough to remember these traumatic events.” - Portland, OR

 “This is a very important project; examining not just JFK’s murder but all four together is especially valuable. The script does this creatively and very well.” – Portland, OR

 “Excellent, powerful play.  Disturbing.  Great piecing together of quotes about the national security state and the assassinations…great way to enter this history.” – Santa Cruz, CA

 “I found myself feeling more and more aghast that this actually happened and was said.” – New York City (from Margot Adler’s Nov. 30th NPR story in which she interviewed the professional actors after their reading of the “Project Unspeakable” play)

“We are very glad we did it. There was nobody who didn’t learn at least something new — in some cases mind-blowing waves of ‘unspeakable’ information and perspective. It was also a community-building exercise for the 12 readers and our wider community.  We would be very interested in doing future readings.” – Cambridge, MA

 “It was a beautiful way to bring all ages and a variety of people, who live in the same city, together to do something constructive — to tell this story that must be told.  Now those readers want to host their own readings in the coming days and months.”  – Brooklyn, NY

 “The conversation afterwards was the best part of the reading. The audience wanted to honestly discuss their thoughts, reservations, etc., and they were excited to have a forum to share.” – Virginia Tech University, VA

 “Our reading was a tremendous success with its very own unique cast of characters. Words were misspoken, names were mispronounced but the spirit of the show was intense and magnetic and affected every single person who was there.” – Idle Hour Café, Nashville, TN

  “[The play brought up] the way you can know and not know, the way you can believe something is true and convince yourself it’s not true because it’s too horrible.” - New York City – from Margot Adler’s Nov. 30th NPR story in which she interviewed the professional actors after their reading of the “Project Unspeakable” play

“Over 300 people came.  Their attention and concern was electric, and the cast was electrified by it … I come away from the experience of participating in this play with renewed determination and hope that we may revive the hopes and dreams of these leaders.” – Leverett, MA

 “Deepest thanks and heartfelt gratitude. Your creative genius managed to hold the narrative of the times and the assassinations in an entirely original and powerful manner.” – Leverett, MA

 “This is not a theatre piece.   It is a courageous and magnificent deed of truth. The frozen ice of untruth and denial could be heard to be cracking, and the spring flood of voices of the people, from the people, rising up out of the abyss, where they have been suffocating these past 5 decades, was flowing out from Leverett on November 22, 2013.” – Leverett, MA
