How To Get Started

Two organizers and a director talk about how they created a public reading in two weeks, and why (video).

Request a script:  Please complete the Contact Us form and request a script in the Message section. We offer the script in different lengths (from 20 minute introduction, 45 minutes, 90 minutes, and two versions of the full 130-minute script).

1. Choose the Type of Production:  Consider using the Project Unspeakable script in one of the following three ways:

a)     A “Living Room Reading”:  invite a group of friends or other guests, send them a script with their parts assigned, and have them highlight their parts in the script so they won’t have to search for them as they read.  Then have them read over their own sections to be familiar with them before the reading.  Finally, all gather, read through and discuss.

You make a dozen or so copies of the script and contact at least twelve local actors and/or friends to be readers.  We have broken the script into twelve roles with each covering several parts.  You will need to assign the roles to the readers and ask them:

  • to come prepared at the time and place of your choosing to read the whole play together;
  • to highlight their sections before the reading, so they don’t have to search for them during the reading itself;
  • to read over their sections before the reading, so that they are familiar with their own parts (there is no requirement for readers to pre-read the whole script, unless they choose to do so).  This is important so that the reading itself doesn’t drag, and so that the readers will be free to listen to what others are reading.

You will then need to arrange for someone (or yourself) to facilitate a post- reading discussion among the participants.  (We will provide you with some suggested discussion questions and facilitation tips.)  Please remember that a Living Room Reading can be attended by other people besides the readers –friends and neighbors who listen and discuss without reading themselves.

b)     A “Readers Theater” production, where with one or two (or more if you choose) rehearsals, a group of readers publicize a staged reading, and invite an audience to witness the event, and then discuss it.  Readers might be a mix of actors, community leaders, activists or other interested parties.  The rehearsals will bring the reading to a level of polish that will make it worthy of audience attention. You may want to invite a “director” to help make the reading as effective as time allows.  You might also include visual media and/or music to enhance the production.  Check with Project Unspeakable to see if materials are available.

c)     A full production of the play.  This would mean mounting the production in any way that feels creative and effective for the company, director, etc.  It is also possible that theater organizations wanting to consider a full production might want to do either 1) or 2) above on 11/22/13, and then use that event as a test run for a possible full production in the next season, or whenever.

Please remember that we are asking all groups to contact us before you begin to use the script for any event.  Depending on the type of event you are planning, we have a simple agreement form that we will ask you to sign and return to us, either by email, or by fax or snail mail.

2. Get a Location:

This reading can be on any scale:  in a living room with friends, in a community center or gymnasium, on the steps of city hall, in a theatre, in a school auditorium -Anywhere!  If there’s already a reading scheduled for your city, either decide to produce your own, additional readings (the more, the merrier), or get in touch with the Project Coordinator and unite your efforts. It’s a great way to make a new group of friends.

3. Pick a time:

Project Unspeakable opens with the 50th anniversary of the assassination of President John F. Kennedy, on November 22, 2013.  We are hoping to have as many readings as possible on or around that date.  If you are interested in looking over the script for a later production, why not convene a Living Room Reading on or close to 11/22/13 so that we can count your reading as a way of building momentum for the project around the country while you give it a read?  We will keep track of your readings and performances and post them on our website as they are scheduled.  The script will be available for readings and performances through 2/21/15, the 50th anniversary of Malcolm X’s assassination (or the end of Black History Month on 2/28/15).

The following is a list of birthdays and anniversaries of John F. Kennedy, Martin Luther King, Malcolm X, and Robert F. Kennedy:

November 22, 2014 – 50th anniversary of JFK’s assassination in 1963

  • January 15, 2014 – MLK’s birthday (born in 1929)
  • February 21, 2014 – anniversary of Malcolm X’s assassination in 1965
  • April 4, 2014 – anniversary of MLK’s assassination in 1968
  • May 19, 2014 – Malcolm X’s birthday (born in 1925)
  • May 29, 2014 – JFK’s birthday (born in 1917)
  • June 6, 2014 – anniversary of RFK’s assassination in 1968
  • November 20, 2014 – RFK’s birthday (born in 1925)
  • November 22, 2014 – 51st anniversary of JFK’s assassination
  • February 21, 2015 – 50th anniversary of Malcolm X’s assassination in 1965

These are ideal dates to hold your production, but you are not limited to these.  Consider when the most people will attend.

4. Cast the roles:

Cast your friends. Cast local celebrities. Cast people that would love the opportunity. Cast theatre critics! Or maybe a national celebrity grew up in your home town — invite them back to read a role. Or have them send a letter to be read at the event. Click here for our suggestions on casting and directing (under “Producing an Event” menu).

5.  Get help: 

In addition to your cast, send an email around asking for volunteers to help you. Keep an email address book of everyone who writes you with interest. You’ll be surprised by how many people want to DO SOMETHING. Project Unspeakable is a great way to form new relationships and work together for the common good. Remember to be inclusive: Find a way for everyone who offers to get involved. Help others realize their dreams.

6.  Make the Project Visible:

Send out press releases (printable templates available soon on the website).  Get people there. Posters, fliers, radio interviews, sandwich boards … Get creative with your cast, friends, and volunteers to promote this production.  Enlist the help of a publicist friend. Make contact with a local representative of your chosen charity, and invite them to get involved personally. Get their mailing lists/emailing lists, and let them help you with publicity.

Recruit someone to take photographs, or to make short videos, or otherwise document the event and share it with us.  We’re hoping to build momentum here, through our website, www.projectunspeakable.com, our Facebook page, and word of mouth, and would love to have you and others be seen as a part of that.

Even if your publicity efforts are minimal or non-existent, your participation in this event is vital to unfolding the truth. We will include your reading in our press releases. So please don’t let “Fear of PR” stop you from producing a reading! Do a small reading in a living room. The collective energy of tiny readings all over the world will be felt.

7.  Send us the details of your production: 

When you receive the script, you will be asked to follow a link on our website to let us know basics, e.g., so we can include it on our website and in our press releases. We need to know your city, state, country, reading location & time, your name, and a contact number and email address.

8.  Contact Us:

Please contact us by email at projectunspeakable@gmail.com to let us know of your interest.  We will add your name to the database and begin the process of sending the script and related materials. If you can, please take photographs, make short videos, or otherwise document the event and share it with us.  We’re hoping to build momentum here, through our website, www.projectunspeakable.com, our Facebook page, and word of mouth, and would love to have you and others be seen as a part of that.

Let us know what else we can do to help you get your reading off the ground.

Always feel free to call us at 413-624-8858 with any questions, or email projectunspeakable@gmail.com
