Appearance at The Shabazz Center in Harlem, NY

We are preparing for our appearance on Saturday at the Malcolm X and Dr. Betty Shabazz Center. Here are some particulars:

Project Unspeakable

a new play by Court Dorsey
Produced & Developed by Liz Stanton & Jeremy Williams
For the Malcolm X 50th Anniversary Memorial
The Malcolm X & Dr. Betty Shabazz Memorial and Educational Center
3940 Broadway New York City, NY 10032
February 21, 2015

It is very exciting that we will be sharing the program with some of Malcolm’s family members, including Malcolm’s daughter, Ilyasah Shabazz, as well as with Dr. James Turner, Al Hajj Imam Talib Abdur-Rashid, who invited us to be on the program, and A. Peter Bailey, a new friend of the Project, and an old friend of Bill Strickland, and of Malcolm’s.  There will also be political dignitaries, the Mayor, as well as other performers and speakers.  It is an honor to be included in this event.

The first two hours, beginning at 2:00 PM, will be streamed live online.  The webcast can be viewed at:

http://www.ebony.com/MalcolmX50thMemorial-221

Although our presentation comes after the simulcast, most of the commemorative section of the program, including an observed silence around 3:10, the time of Minister Malcolm’s assassination 50 years ago, will be broadcast.  As Jim Douglass (JFK and the Unspeakable author) pointed out to me graciously this afternoon, Project Unspeakable has the longest allotted time on the live program at 30 minutes. Our segment will run from 5:00 to 5:30 PM.

Our performance will include live singing by our Motown “Girl-Group” Narrators, as well as a first rough draft of projected images and multi-media.

We are all proud to be a part of this historic event.  I’ll report back to you next week to let you know how it goes.

Bye for now,
Court

Najah Imani Muhammad plays Tenay, the young film-maker who wrestles with the Unspeakable. It is through her that the story is told.
