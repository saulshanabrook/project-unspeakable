Sample Intro to a Reading

Sample of an Intro to Project Unspeakable – 599 words – Thanks to Paula Green for the original, which we adapted.

Good evening one and all. It is my pleasure to welcome everyone here on behalf of _____ and the cast of Project Unspeakable. My name is _____.

We hope this program will encourage more awareness of the consequences of violence and increase advocacy for more constructive methods of responding to conflict at home and abroad. Our story tonight is steeped in violence and may be the only literature linking the assassination of JFK with the those of Malcolm X, MLK, RFK, all killed between 1963 and ’68.

The play examines the visions, values, ethics, and approaches to social change, with special emphasis on the way their ideas and capacities enlarged and deepened as they entered into their leadership roles. The more they developed and the larger became their vision of peace and justice, the more they threatened the status quo and the entrenched institutions.

Much of what is brought before us is dismissed as “conspiracy theory.” Tonight we invite you to open your hearts to a deeper understanding. Focus on connecting the dots and following the inner development and awareness of each man and the consequent threat to their lives. Their triumphant emergence as moral leaders became their death sentence. Why? Who did they threaten? Why have investigations into their murders been hidden? What can we learn from this history for our own times? And why does it matter?

This play is inspired by a book by James Douglass: JFK and the Unspeakable: Why He Died and Why It Matters and much other material. Jim led a workshop at Rowe Conference Center in December of 2012 and that workshop stirred the imaginations of many Western Massachusetts activists, who banded together to produce it.

Special appreciation goes to Court Dorsey, backed up by writers Steve Wangh and Debbie Lynangale, and the staff of Project Unspeakable, to all who helped with production, to James Douglass for his 14 years of research in writing the JFK book, and to my fellow readers for their willingness to bring you this play.

Readings will continue, especially on the birth and death anniversaries of our 4 martyrs, through at least February of 2015, and may continue until 2018.

As you listen, we invite you to attend to the issue of transformation. What happened to JFK and RFK as they confronted the Cuban Missile Crisis and came close to the brink of nuclear war with the Soviet Union? How did Malcolm X transform and what vision was he exploring before his untimely death? Why was Dr. King vulnerable to assassination when he enlarged his public teaching to include opposition to the war in Vietnam as well as economic justice and human rights for African Americans? And what threat did Robert Kennedy pose to the status quo? Why was he also targeted for assassination? What common truths underlined their vision and why were their voices silenced?

The play is in two acts, and takes a while, but those who have heard it before have been deeply moved. Please be comfortable and patient. There will be an intermission, and for those who can stay, a discussion after the second act. A sign-up sheet for future opportunities to discuss these issues will be _______.

Our production is directed by __________ and reading tonight are not actors, but concerned citizen/(or)/ a few actors, but mostly concerned citizens. Our director, _______, will offer a few reflections and then our staged reading will begin. Thank you for being with us on this historic evening.
